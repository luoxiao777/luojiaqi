Donor Archives

2023

Research‑driven publication on donor figures in Chinese and Western painting

Donor Archives is a research‑oriented publication about the evolution of donor figures in painting.

The project compares images of donors from Sui, Tang and Five Dynasties Chinese painting with those in Spanish Renaissance painting, and discusses how these figures carry religious, social and historical meaning.

ABOUT

Donor Archives: a brief description of the evolution of donor images in paintings—exemplified by donor figures in Sui, Tang, and Five Dynasties Chinese painting and Spanish Renaissance painting.

ELIXIR31012

2025

Visual identity and packaging for a London-based accessories brand

ELIXIR31012 is a London‑based accessories brand founded in 2023.
For this project I was responsible for refreshing the logo and developing a visual system that could extend across packaging and other applications.

The brand is co‑founded by 3D artist Xu Jing and sound artist Xia Yiyun. Their practice sits between fashion design, digital animation, sound art, wearable objects and performance, and the visual identity reflects this hybrid, experimental character.

The design explores the relationship between post‑humanism, mysticism and underground club culture. It focuses on the body, consciousness and the perception of time and space, using new media tools and imaginative visuals to express the energy of a young sub‑cultural community.

CYPHER BARBERSHOP

2022

Logo system and visual materials for a community‑driven barbershop

CYPHER Barbershop is a community‑focused barbershop in Xi’an. It is not only a place for haircuts, but also a hub for conversation, shared interests and cultural exchange.

For this project I designed the logo and extended visual system for posters, signage and printed materials. The identity is built around the letter “C”, which stands for culture, circulation and cohesion, echoing the idea of the “cypher” circle in Black culture where people communicate and pass energy between each other.

ABOUT

CYPHER Barbershop was founded in Xi’an in 2022. It is more than just a barbershop; it is a community‑centered hub for connection, a forum for sharing interests, and a space for cultural inheritance.

The name “Cypher” carries three meanings here: culture, transmission, and cohesion. In Black culture, it represents communication and connection between community members. The use of the letter “C” symbolizes openness and inclusiveness, rooted in cultural acceptance and exchange.

Caochiwan Crested Ibis Village

2023

Visual materials for Caochiwan village and its crested ibis conservation project

Caochiwan is a historic village on the southern slope of the Qinling Mountains — a model site for rural revitalization and the first “nature education village” in the region.

For more than ten years, 42 crested ibises have lived in harmony with 52 local households here. The village is quiet and secluded; the birds bring a sense of spirituality and poetry, while contemporary life injects energy and movement.

I worked on the park brochure, event posters, packaging and other visual materials, building a system that connects the imagery of the ibises with the everyday life of the village.

ABOUT

Caochiwan is an ancient village located at the southern foot of the Qinling Mountains. It serves as a model village for rural revitalization in the Qinling region, China and the first village for nature education in the Qinling Mountains, China.

Chun Lai He Restaurant

2023

Branding and visual system for a time‑honoured restaurant

Chun Lai He is a long‑established restaurant in Shaanxi, specialising in fusion cuisine.

The visual identity is built around the question “What is home?” — connecting everyday objects and situations (the dining table, familiar tableware, family gatherings, childhood banquets, the anticipation before a meal, the gesture of raising a glass) with the emotional memory of home.

The bowl, as a basic vessel for serving food, becomes a key symbol. It carries not only eating habits and etiquette, but also collective memory and the idea of reunion and completeness for Chinese families.

ABOUT

Chun Lai He is a time-honored restaurant native to Shaanxi, specializing in fusion cuisine. The design links objects and feelings to memories through the concept of “What is home”.

KPEE BOOKS

2021

Bookstore logo, book design and poetry collection layout

KPEE BOOKS is an independent bookstore and publishing project in Chengdu. Since its opening in July 2020 on Yujie Alley, the space has combined book retail, small‑scale publishing and public events.

I worked on multiple book covers and interior layouts, as well as the bookstore’s logo and visual identity.

One of the key projects is Sad Chicken, a poetry collection by Wu Qing published in August 2021 (245 pages). It is his second anthology of short poems after How White the Clouds Are in the Sky, spanning nearly a decade of writing.

ABOUT

Serving the coolest readers and publishing books for the most brilliant talents. Established in July 2020, the physical space of KPEE Bookstore was located on Yujie Alley, Chengdu. It was involved in book design as well as the bookstore’s logo design.

Sad Chicken is a poetry collection published by the poet Wu Qing in August 2021, with a total of 245 pages. It is Wu Qing’s second anthology of selected short poems following How White the Clouds Are in the Sky, covering a writing span of nearly a decade.
